# THE CONFLUENCE / REWRITING WALL — A Formal Map

The third wall, given the parity-grade PROVEN/VERIFIED/CITED/OBSTRUCTED/OPEN treatment. Its distinguishing feature: the
core is **OBSTRUCTED (undecidable)**, not OPEN — making it a *different kind* of wall than parity (parity∈P?) and SAT
(P vs NP), and so a complementary asset. It also ties directly to III's own XII rewriting engine.

**Proof-status legend** as the parity capstone. **Honesty invariant (math-olympiad #4):** grounding the decidable island
says nothing about the undecidable general case; no island claim crosses the obstruction.

---

## 1. Object and ground truth

An **abstract rewriting system (ARS)** is a relation `→` on a set; a **term-rewriting system (TRS)** is the special
case where elements are terms and `→` is generated by rewrite rules. Key predicates: **terminating** (no infinite
`→`-chain), **confluent** (any two reducts of an element have a common reduct), **locally confluent** (any two *one-step*
reducts join), **normal form** (an irreducible element). The **oracle** for a *finite* ARS is direct computation
(reflexive-transitive closure, normal forms) — used in `1868`/`1869`.

---

## 2. The decidable island, and its sharp boundary

- **Newman's Lemma (island, VERIFIED `1868`).** For a **terminating** ARS, the three predicates coincide: global
  confluence `⟺` local confluence `⟺` unique normal forms. Verified on 6000 random terminating systems (both confluent
  and non-confluent). Because local confluence (critical-pair peaks) and unique normal forms are poly-checkable,
  **confluence of a terminating system is DECIDABLE** — the basis of Knuth–Bendix completion (CITED).
- **The boundary (PROVEN `1869`).** Newman's lemma *requires* termination: the witness `{a⇄b, a→c, b→d}` (c,d normal)
  is **locally confluent** but **not globally confluent** and **non-terminating**. Drop termination and the island
  collapses — local confluence no longer implies confluence. This is the wall's sharp edge (the analog of parity's T3 /
  SAT's Schaefer cliff): *termination is exactly what makes confluence decidable*.
- **Critical pairs (CITED, Knuth–Bendix 1970).** For a terminating TRS, local confluence is decided by checking that
  every *critical pair* (overlap of two rule left-hand sides) joins — a finite, mechanical test. Ground-term confluence
  is decidable even without termination (CITED, Dauchet–Tison).

---

## 3. The obstructed core (the wall itself)

- **OBSTRUCTED (undecidable).** Confluence of a **general** (possibly non-terminating) term-rewriting system is
  **undecidable** — **CITED** (a classical consequence; reductions from the halting/Post correspondence problems).
  Termination itself is undecidable (CITED). So the wall is not "open" like parity/SAT — it is a *proven* impossibility:
  no algorithm decides confluence of arbitrary TRS. The decidable island (terminating systems, §2) is the most one can
  have; the boundary (`1869`) shows the island cannot be extended past termination.

---

## 4. Cross-wall position — walls come in KINDS

This wall sharpens the cross-wall asset by adding a *second kind of core*:

| Wall | Core | Core status | Island | Boundary |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Parity | parity ∈ P? | **OPEN** | bounded-d / 1-player (`1857`/`1850`) | the 1→2-player / `d=Θ(n)` step |
| SAT | P vs NP | **OPEN** | Schaefer's six classes (`1863`–`1867`) | the Schaefer cliff (3-SAT) |
| **Confluence** | decide confluence of any TRS | **OBSTRUCTED (undecidable)** | terminating systems (`1868`) | termination (`1869`) |

So the landscape has (at least) two kinds of wall: **OPEN-core** (the frontier is unknown — parity, SAT) and
**OBSTRUCTED-core** (the frontier is a *proven* impossibility — confluence, and from the grail ledger: general-TRS
confluence, impredicative ordinal analysis, Rice/halting). Every wall, of *either* kind, has the same internal shape: a
**decidable/tractable island**, a **sharp boundary** that names the island's essential precondition, and a **core** past
it. The essential precondition differs by wall — *control/alternation* (parity), *clause structure* (SAT), *termination*
(confluence) — but the *pattern* is invariant. That invariance is the compounding asset: it is the template, confirmed
now on three structurally different walls.

## 5. Apply-to-real-III — XII lives on the island

III's own canonicaliser **XII** is a term-rewriting system (40 rules, `DOCS/III-XII.md`). It is engineered to be
**terminating** (a well-founded weight order) *and* **confluent** (critical pairs all join), so by Newman's Lemma (§2)
its confluence is *decidable* and *decided* — it sits squarely on the tractable island, by construction. This is not a
coincidence but the design discipline the island licenses: build your rewriting on the decidable side of the boundary
(`1869`), and confluence becomes a checkable property rather than an undecidable hope. The confluence wall is therefore
not abstract to III — it is the theory under III's own canonical-form guarantees.
