## The five rules (this is the whole language)

1. A claim is `[ thing verb thing ]`.
2. The verbs: `=` **is** (identity) · `<` **under** (order) · `~` **mirrors** (dual).
3. A thing is a **phrase** — words and exact counts (`9`, or `124:1766`) in
   sequence — or a claim in brackets.  A nested claim is **mentioned**, not
   stated (the language can talk about its own claims lawfully).
4. Claims sit side by side in any order.  Every scroll has **one canonical
   spelling**: same knowledge → same bytes → same address.  This is the
   (1,1,1) law: the shape *is* the address *is* the value.
5. The unraveling: `=` joins (congruence); `<` chains and never cycles; `~`
   pairs uniquely (a second mirror of one thing *is* an equality) and
   **reverses** `<` (the order-reversing involution).  Nothing else is ever
   assumed.

Read aloud: `[dream ~ wake]` — *dream mirrors wake*.
`[kardia 124:1766 = derived]` — *kardia, at 124 of 1766, is derived*.
`[belief 9:12 < assert]` — *belief, at nine of twelve, sits under assert*.
