# THE LATTICE-REPRESENTATION WALL — A Formal Map

The fifth wall (user-named), parity-grade. Its open core is the **Finite Lattice Representation Problem (FLRP)** —
unmoved since the 1960s — and it contributes a *new flavor of boundary* to the cross-wall taxonomy: **finiteness**.

**Proof-status legend** as the parity capstone. **Honesty invariant (math-olympiad #4):** realizing specific lattices is
the island; it does not settle the universal (FLRP) open core.

---

## 1. Object and ground truth

An **algebra** `A` is a set with operations; a **congruence** is an equivalence relation on `A` compatible with every
operation. The congruences, ordered by refinement, form the **congruence lattice** `Con(A)`. The **oracle** for a finite
algebra: enumerate equivalence relations and test compatibility (the partitions are generated by restricted-growth
strings; `1873`/`1874`). The question of the wall: which lattices *are* congruence lattices?

---

## 2. The substrate and the island

- **The substrate (VERIFIED `1874`).** The partitions of a finite set form a **lattice** `Π_n` (meet = common
  refinement = intersection of the equivalence relations; join = transitive closure of their union). All lattice axioms
  — idempotent, commutative, associative, absorption, and meet/join staying equivalences — verified on all 15 partitions
  of a 4-set. Since `Con(A)` is exactly the partitions compatible with `A`'s operations, **`Con(A)` is a sublattice of
  `Π_n`** — *this is why congruences always form a lattice*, the foundation under the whole wall.
- **The island — non-distributive lattices ARE realizable (PROVEN `1873`).** `Con(V₄) ≅ M₃`: the congruence lattice of
  the Klein four-group `(ℤ₂×ℤ₂, XOR)` is exactly `M₃`, the 5-element diamond and **smallest non-distributive lattice**
  (bottom, three incomparable atoms = the cosets of the three order-2 subgroups, top; `meet=⊥`, `join=⊤` pairwise). So a
  non-distributive lattice is the congruence lattice of a finite algebra — representability is *non-trivial*.
- **More islands (CITED).** Every finite **distributive** lattice is a congruence lattice of a finite algebra (a
  standard representation). Every finite lattice **embeds** into a finite partition lattice (Pudlák–Tůma 1980) — but
  *embedding* is weaker than being `Con(A)`.

---

## 3. The boundary, and the open core — finiteness

- **The boundary is FINITENESS (CITED, Grätzer–Schmidt 1963).** Every finite lattice (indeed every *algebraic* lattice)
  **is** the congruence lattice of *some* algebra — but the algebra may be **infinite**. So the obstruction is not
  *whether* a lattice can be realized, but whether it can be realized over a **finite** algebra. The wall sits exactly at
  the finite/infinite boundary — a precondition unlike any prior wall's.
- **OPEN core — FLRP.** Is **every finite lattice** the congruence lattice of a **finite** algebra? — **OPEN.** A famous,
  long-standing problem of universal algebra (equivalent, by a McKenzie/Pálfy–Pudlák line, to questions about
  transitive subgroup lattices / finite groups). The islands (`1873`/`1874`) realize specific and structured lattices;
  the universal question is the wall.

---

## 4. Cross-wall position — a fourth kind of boundary

This wall extends the taxonomy. The "essential precondition" that separates a wall's island from its core has now
appeared in four distinct flavors across five walls:

| Wall | Core status | Essential precondition (the boundary) |
|---|---|---|
| Parity | OPEN | **control / alternation** (1→2 players, `d=Θ(n)`) |
| SAT | OPEN | **clause structure** (Schaefer's six classes) |
| Confluence | OBSTRUCTED | **termination** (Newman) |
| Graph iso | OPEN | **structural depth** (1-WL→`k`-WL) |
| **Lattice rep** | OPEN | **finiteness** (Grätzer–Schmidt: infinite is free) |

The invariant island/boundary/core *template* holds on all five; the *precondition* differs every time. That the
precondition is wall-specific while the template is universal is the compounding lesson — the lattice wall adds
"finiteness" to the list, a boundary no other wall here exhibits.

**Bottom line.** The lattice-representation wall is an OPEN-core wall whose island already realizes a *non-distributive*
lattice as a congruence lattice (`Con(V₄)=M₃`, `1873`) over the substrate that congruences always form a lattice
(`1874`), and whose boundary is the singular **finiteness** condition (Grätzer–Schmidt makes infinite algebras free).
Five walls mapped on one template — parity, SAT, confluence, graph-isomorphism, lattice representation — each a grounded
asset, together a taxonomy of how tractable islands sit against open or obstructed cores, separated by a wall-specific
essential precondition.
