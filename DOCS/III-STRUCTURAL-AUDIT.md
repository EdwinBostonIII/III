# 1. Module and File Combination (consolidation)

The strongest single architectural finding hiding inside this audit is not any one defect. It is that **the same handful of primitives are hand rolled independently in a dozen places**, and the cross cutting lever series is, read sideways, a list of missing shared organs. The combination opportunities therefore split into two tiers: a small set of *missing primitive layers* that would absorb many call sites at once, and a set of *direct duplications* that should simply be merged.

## 1A. The missing primitive layer (the high leverage tier)

**COMBINE-1 · The NTT convolution organ.** This is the audit's flagship consolidation, `X1`, and it is both a combination and a separation at once. The Number Theoretic Transform, the exact integer sibling of the quantum Fourier transform, already exists *three times* inside III: inside `mlkem` and `mldsa` (the lattice post quantum modules, finding E-MLK-1 / E-MLD-1) and again inside `zk_stark` (F-ST-1). The lever asks for one `numera/ntt.iii` over a fixed prime with static twiddle tables, with everything large enough routed through it: very large bigint multiply above a measured threshold (D-KARA-3, D-BI-2), polynomial and Galois field polynomial products, and any large cyclic convolution (F-CORE-5, D-MOD-2). The honest ceiling matters and is part of the consolidation: it must *not* swallow the small operand cases, because below roughly a few thousand bits schoolbook reduction is faster, which is why `fp256`, `fp384`, and `fn256` stay on their current path. The win is that one audited, sealed convolution primitive replaces three independent transforms and unlocks the asymptotic improvement for the large operand regime that today is silently broken (see RIPPLE-2 and ENHANCE-2).
> **[CORRECTED 2026-05-29]** The census is an **undercount**: a *fourth* NTT exists at `STDLIB/iii/numera/entropy_monitor.iii::entropy_spectrum` (Cooley-Tukey over `GF(998244353)`, its own modular stack). It **shares the prime 998244353 with `zk_stark`**. Corrected scope: **four NTTs over three primes**, one shared (998244353) twiddle table consumed twice. `entropy_monitor.iii` is a fourth consolidation target.

**COMBINE-2 · One fast non cryptographic hash for the bucketing paths.** Lever `X2`. The system conflates two genuinely different needs: cryptographic collision resistance, which belongs only on *seals* and integrity digests, and cheap bucketing for hash tables, which needs no such strength because correctness is guaranteed by a full key comparison on every probe. The headline instance is F-CORE-2: the egraph, the load bearing optimization engine, runs cryptographic Keccak-256 on its hashcons inner loop, hundreds of cycles per node, when a 64 bit integer mix would do, for an expected ten to fifty times speedup on the optimizer's hottest path. `murmur3.iii` is already in the tree, seventy lines, unused for this. The consolidation is to route every *bucketing* hash (F-CORE-2 in `egraph`, D-MLC-1 in `math_library_curation`) onto the one fast hash and reserve Keccak and SHA strictly for seals. Notice the elegance: this is simultaneously a combination (one fast hash) and a removal of misplaced cryptography from hot paths.

**COMBINE-3 · A shared content address index to retire the O(N^2) all pairs scans.** Lever `X3`, and it recurs more than any other efficiency pattern in the document. Again and again a module compares every pair of items, an O(N^2) scan, when it could bucket by an exact key in close to linear time. The audit frames this as the deterministic superposition collapse, grouping equal states into one class. The instances are striking in their spread: `congruence` (the INTERN lever), `theorem_carrier` (C-TC-3, ids are already 32 byte content hashes), `math_library_curation` (D-MLC-1, the ticket id is already a Keccak hash), `ripple_metric` (A-RM-2), `ripple_loop` (A-RL-3), `computation_graph` (F-CG-4), `unify` (J-UNIFY-2), `lru` (J-LRU-1), and the compiler's own `sema` name resolution (G-SEMA-1, which is O(N^2 times L) today). The combination is one deterministic open addressed hash index primitive, static BSS with a power of two mask, that these call sites share. The exemplar to copy already exists and is confirmed correct: `COMPILER/BOOT/ast.iii` is a textbook content addressed Merkle DAG with SHA-256 hash consing (G-AST-QPA), and the audit explicitly names it the index that the X3 lever should point other modules toward.

**COMBINE-4 · Unified modular reduction (Montgomery and Barrett).** Levers `X5` and `X13`. Reduction modulo a fixed or repeated modulus is reimplemented per file, and several of those reimplementations are wrong or slow. The most defect dense file in the entire system, `modular_mont`, carries two Critical reduction bugs (MONT-1, the REDC overflow, and MONT-2, the even modulus garbage inverse). Meanwhile `field` does a fresh bit serial long division reduction on every multiply (D-FLD-1), `bigint_div` and `modular` would benefit from a fixed modulus dispatch (D-MOD-1), and on the crypto hot paths the special form folds are missing entirely: `x25519` uses generic division where the `2^255 minus 19` fold would be roughly a thousand times faster (E-X-1, and note it should simply *delegate to* `fe25519`'s field rather than carry its own, which is COMBINE-9), and the post quantum NTT loops divide per butterfly where Montgomery on the fixed primes `3329` and `8380417` removes every division (E-MLK-2, E-MLD-2). The consolidation is one reduction layer with the overflow fixed at the source, offered in a Montgomery form for repeated moduli and a special form fold for the named primes, with `bigint_mod` kept only as the generic fallback.

**COMBINE-5 · `omnia/pq` as the one tropical selection substrate.** Lever `X11`. The min plus, or tropical, semiring (where addition is the usual sum and the choice operator is minimum) is the exact classical form of quantum interference selecting an optimum, and III performs that selection in several disconnected places. The audit names `omnia/pq` the canonical home and asks that the egraph's min cost extraction (F-CORE-3, today a Bellman and Ford full relaxation to fixpoint) be recast as a priority ordered Dijkstra style worklist *over `pq`*, and that any Viterbi or min plus dynamic program route through the same substrate. The combination is real and it has a prerequisite: `pq` itself should first be made maximally efficient (A-PQ-1, the hole sift technique to cut its memory traffic by roughly three times), because every consumer inherits its constant factor.

**COMBINE-6 · One deterministic tie break authority.** Lever `X21`, and `numera/tiebreak.iii` already *is* this organ, fully implemented and clean. It encodes the canonical deterministic ordering: primary by `u64`, then by 256 bit identity in lexical order, then by least index, with null and empty guards that are proven to fire. Yet the same ordering is hand rolled all over Volume I: the egraph extraction tie break (F-CORE-3), `eg_union`'s lower id rule, the ripple Kahn topological order, and `rs_argmax`'s least index rule each reinvent it. The combination is to route those scattered ad hoc tie breaks through the one audited authority. This is the cleanest kind of consolidation, because the destination module already exists and is correct; the work is only redirection.

**COMBINE-7 · A boundary contract helper, the synthesis of `X6`, `X7`, `X18`, `X19`, `X22`, `X23`, `X24` (inferred).** This is the deepest combination insight in the document and the audit never states it as one, because it spread the evidence across seven separate levers. Read them together and they are a single thing: III has no enforced trust discipline at its module exports. Every `@export` that takes a caller supplied length or id either masks the id modularly so an out of range value silently aliases a live slot (`X19`: D-QF-1, K-OBST-1, K-SBQ-1, I-FT, A-RP-4, II-ASYNC-1), adds a length unchecked so adversarial input wraps past 2^64 and defeats the capacity guard (`X18` and `X22`: J-LIST-1, A-PQ-3, the entire `tp_*` codec family, and most severely the remote reachable `http_server` and `http_client` where untrusted network bytes drive an out of bounds write, II-HTTPSERVER-1/2), overloads its zero sentinel so "table full" is indistinguishable from "no error" (`X6`: D-SP-2, D-CHK-1, D-CC-1, J-OPT-1), or silently discards a callee's error return so a truncated or failed operation reports success (`X23` and `X24`: A-CG-1, II-REACH_ORACLE-2, II-BASE32-1, II-FORMAT-1, II-INET-1). These are not five hundred separate bugs; they are one absent layer. The combination opportunity is a thin shared set of boundary helpers, a bounded index check, a subtraction form capacity guard that cannot wrap, a distinct sentinel convention, and a propagated error idiom, that every export routes through. This single layer would close the largest defect class in the entire codebase, and it is the architectural counterpart of the audit's per file guards.

## 1B. Direct duplications to merge

**COMBINE-8 · `checked` duplicates `omnia/option`'s u64 table.** Finding D-CHK-2 states it plainly: `checked` carries a u64 table that is a duplicate of the one in `omnia/option`, and recommends they be unified. Small, concrete, no design decision required.

**COMBINE-9 · `x25519` should consume `fe25519`'s field rather than carry its own reduction.** Finding E-X-1 again. `x25519` reimplements reduction modulo `2^255 minus 19` with generic bit serial division, roughly a thousand times slower than the special form fold that its sibling `fe25519` already implements correctly. The merge is to have `x25519` delegate to `fe25519`'s eight limb field, which also resolves the constant time concern E-X-3 by moving onto a data independent field. One implementation of the curve's field arithmetic, not two.

**COMBINE-10 · `murmur3` should reuse the shared endian helper.** Finding D-MUR-1: `murmur3` hand rolls its little endian load where `endian_load_u32_le` already exists. Trivial, but it is the same single source of truth principle at the smallest scale.

**COMBINE-11 · `sat_arith` should delegate to `scalar`'s saturating operations.** Finding D-SA-1: `sat_arith` double evaluates its arithmetic, computing the predicate and the result separately, where `scalar` already offers single pass saturating operations. Merge the logic onto `scalar`.

**COMBINE-12 · The `kernel.iii` reference resolves to `typecheck.iii`.** Finding II-ST-1. This is a documentation level merge, but worth listing because a dangling reference reads as a missing subsystem. The `safety_type` header claims the full kernel "is completed by the H13 in language `numera/kernel.iii` port." No such file exists. `typecheck.iii`, at 2,959 lines, *is* that kernel. The action is to repoint the header reference so the two names denote one thing.

**COMBINE-13 · The two implementations of crypto, the shadow problem (inferred, and see CUT-12, SEPARATE-5).** This is the most consequential duplication in the system, and the audit surfaces it under `X25` as a scaffold and security finding rather than as a consolidation. III has crypto *twice*: the real, KAT verified `numera/crypt_ed25519`, `numera/x25519` and `fe25519`, `numera/keccak`, `numera/blake2s`, and `numera/crc32` from Volume I, and a second set of "curated" LDIL machine code inlines in the `xii_curated_*` cluster that are constant returning stubs (II-XCF-1: the curated `ed25519_verify` returns a fixed "valid" mask unconditionally because it compares a register with itself). The override mechanism makes the stubs win. The correct resolution is the single source of truth move: the inlines should be *generated from* the proven implementations, so the inline literally is the verified algorithm, or the override registration should be removed so emission falls back to the real routines. Either way, one source of crypto truth, not two, with the stub set never the live one.

The live security hole is **closed**. Remaining work is now *completion* (generate inlines from the proven `numera` crypto, or make the chokepoint permanent + a falsifier KAT), not an emergency.

# 2. Module and File Separation (splitting)

As noted, this is the thinnest axis, and honesty about that is part of the answer: the NIH discipline already over divides, so most files should *not* be split. The genuine candidates are the few large files where the audit's own findings reveal distinct concerns bundled together, plus one structural question that dwarfs the rest.

**SEPARATE-1 · `egraph.iii` bundles six concerns and the audit's three core findings each touch a different one.** This is the best grounded split candidate. The file is 1,628 lines and the audit describes it as union find, open address hashcons, bounded equality saturation, min cost extraction, a Keccak Merkle seal, and a GF(2^8) Reed Solomon self heal, all in one place. Now look at how the findings cleave it: F-CORE-1 (deferred rebuild) lives in the saturation and union concern, F-CORE-2 (drop Keccak from the bucketing) lives precisely at the seam between the hashcons concern, which needs only a fast hash, and the *seal* concern, which genuinely needs Keccak's collision resistance, and F-CORE-3 (Dijkstra extraction) lives in the extraction concern and wants to move onto `pq` (COMBINE-5). The separation that falls out: extract the integrity layer, the seal and the Reed Solomon self heal, into its own file so the cryptographic dependency is isolated from the superposition engine. This makes F-CORE-2 clean (the fast hash lives with the egraph, Keccak lives with the seal), it clarifies that the engine and its integrity audit are different responsibilities, and it shrinks the part of the file you must reason about when changing optimization behavior. The audit hints at the seam without naming the split.

**SEPARATE-2 · The proof kernel's trusted base is drawn in the wrong place. (The single most important separation in the system.)** This is the deep one, and the audit raises it directly in II-TC-1 and the kernel cluster synthesis. The narrative claim is that `typecheck` is the sole arbiter of meaning. The reality, after "NbE fully deleted, this is the ONLY conversion path," is that `typecheck` delegates its entire trusted computational base to `ccl` (898 lines) plus the `tc_to_ccl` and `ccl_to_tc` translation, so the true trusted base is the large reducer and the translation layer, not the checker. The header's own thesis, that the smaller the trusted base the stronger the de Bruijn criterion, is in tension with that delegation. The separation opportunity is conceptual before it is physical: draw the trusted base boundary explicitly and minimally. Concretely, that means isolating the reducer and the translation as a sealed, exhaustively certified unit, keeping the second independent conversion oracle (`combinator.iii`'s `cb_conv`) as a *permanent* differential check so a bug in `ccl` must also fool the combinator path to escape (ENHANCE-21), and making confluence a theorem rather than a sample by wiring the already present `xii_critpair_enum` and `xii_conf_cert` to the CCL rule set (II-CCL-3). The audit records that the four soundness items here (II-CCL-1, 2, 3 and II-TC-2) were closed on 2026-05-29, which is exactly why the boundary now deserves to be drawn cleanly: the logic is sound, so the architecture should make that soundness legible and defended rather than implicit in a large undivided reducer.

**SEPARATE-3 · The cost field is a stub fused into `sov_isa`'s wiring. (inferred, grounded in F-CORE-4.)** `sov_isa.iii` (795 lines) wires the egraph, the cost lattice, the proof kernel, and the contingent evolution driver together. Finding F-CORE-4 shows that the six dimensional micro architectural cost field, the headline of the whole calculus, "differential descent over the cost field," is set to near constant values, every dimension a one. The cost model is logically a separate concern from the wiring that consumes it, and the source for a real model already exists in `microarch_model.iii`. The separation is to give the cost field its own clearly bounded home (populated from `microarch_model`) so that the descent has a genuine gradient and the wiring file stops carrying a placeholder model. This is a separation that exists chiefly to enable an enhancement (ENHANCE-4).

**SEPARATE-4 · `cg_r3.iii` could split its proof witness concern from its lowering concern. (inferred, low confidence, grounded in MATCH-SLOT-1 and PCC-1.)** The Ring-3 code generator is the largest file touched at 3,384 lines. Two findings sit in it: MATCH-SLOT-1 (a local count overrun in the lowering logic) and PCC-1 (the SHA-256 emit witness comparison, confirmed correct, no change). These are different responsibilities, lowering and proof carrying self attestation. I flag this with deliberate low confidence: code generation is inherently large, the file is otherwise clean, and the audit does not ask for the split. List it only as a candidate to revisit if maintenance friction on that file grows, not as a mandate. The same caution applies, even more weakly, to `smt.iii` (1,984 lines, where C-SMT-1 threaded an overflow flag through twelve sites) and `typecheck.iii` itself.

**SEPARATE-5 · The `xii_curated_*` override registration should be split from the curated bodies. (inferred, grounded in the X25 chokepoint.)** The crypto scaffold problem (COMBINE-13, CUT-12) is fixed at a single chokepoint: `xii_emit_gen._find_override` was made to return nothing for crypto horizons so they fall through to the real kernel. That fix is elegant precisely because the *registration* (which body wins) is separable from the *bodies* themselves. The separation worth making permanent is to keep the override *policy*, the decision of when a curated inline may take precedence, in one small auditable place, distinct from the catalog of inline bodies. That way the dangerous coupling, a stub silently outranking a real implementation, cannot be reintroduced by adding a body; it can only be reintroduced by editing the one policy file, which is exactly where scrutiny belongs.

# 3. Module and File Interactions and Ripples

This is the richest axis, because the audit's lever series is, in effect, a partial dependency map. The opportunities here are of three kinds: *fix once and many heal* (a shared dependency whose correction propagates), *edit here and that must rebuild* (the seal and forge cascades), and *change this and watch those* (the dispatch tables and lifecycle couplings). Understanding these is what keeps a fix from becoming a regression.

## 3A. The soundness cascade (the master ripple)

**RIPPLE-1 · The kernel funnel: `ccl` and `typecheck` carry the soundness of everything that proves anything.** This is the single most important interaction in the system and the audit states it in full in the kernel cluster synthesis. Everything that "reduces to the kernel," and that is a long list, is sound if and only if the kernel is: `induct` (the universal quantifier bridge, which is exactly as sound as `tc_natrec` and `tc_check`), `safety_type` (the M9 apotheosis module), `aeu` and `integrity` (the phi admission predicate), and crucially every optimizer pipeline in Volume I that follows the "propose, then the kernel disposes" pattern, `sov_pipeline` and all the proof carrying gates. The ripple direction is downward from `ccl`: the four soundness fixes (II-CCL-1 read back, II-CCL-2 fail closed exhaustion, II-CCL-3 exhaustive confluence, II-TC-2 fail open context overflow) propagate correctness to that entire dependent set. The audit records all four as landed and gated green on 2026-05-29, which closes the cascade. The lasting lesson for future work: any change to the reducer is a change to the meaning of every proof in the system, so the reducer is the one place where the cost of a mistake is unbounded, and the differential oracle (ENHANCE-21) exists to bound it.

## 3B. Fix once and many heal (shared dependency corrections)

**RIPPLE-2 · The NTT organ heals the silently broken large operand multiply.** Building COMBINE-1, observe the *interaction*: finding D-KARA-1 shows that today, for large operands (around eight thousand limbs), the Karatsuba slot table exhausts, `bigint_new` returns the INVALID value zero, and zero is then silently treated as a zero bigint, producing a wrong or truncated product with no error, in exactly the large operand regime Karatsuba exists to serve. The NTT tier (D-KARA-3) does not merely add speed; it *resolves the correctness break*, because the transform does not allocate per recursion the way the recursive split does. So COMBINE-1 ripples into a correctness fix in `bigint_karatsuba`, and through `bigint` into every consumer of very large multiplication. One organ, two effects.

**RIPPLE-3 · `crystal.iii` is a shared minting authority and changes to it touch every crystal user.** Several modules mint "crystals," the system's provenance and witness objects: `scalar_provenance` (D-SP-1, which corrupts provenance chains by passing an operand value where a parent id belongs), `field_crystal` (D-FC-1 and FC-COLLIDE-1, where success ids collide with crystal ids), `checked_crystal` (D-CC-1), and `q128_f64`'s round direction crystal. The audit flags the ripple explicitly: the clean fix, minting crystals into a disjoint high id band, "touches shared `crystal.iii`" and therefore every consumer. The resolution chosen, `CRYSTAL_ID_BASE` at `0x10000`, is transparent precisely because all consumers resolve through `crystal_slot_of`, which is the right property for a shared authority. The interaction principle: id allocation is a system wide contract, and a per file fix to a collision is the wrong altitude; the fix belongs in the shared minter.
> **[CORRECTED 2026-05-29]** Path: the shared minter is **`STDLIB/iii/omnia/crystal.iii`** (not `numera/crystal.iii`). All four consumers and the `crystal_slot_of` / `CRYSTAL_ID_BASE 0x10000` mechanism are confirmed; only the path is misstated.

**RIPPLE-4 · `congruence.iii` underpins the entire `forcefield/ripple_*` family.** The ripple modules sit on `congruence`'s `cgr_*` ring and on the commit gate's `cg_decide`. The most instructive interaction is A-RX-1 and A-RX-2: `ripple_extract`'s capability audit probe *permanently interns the address it is auditing*, mutating the very ring it inspects, so its verdict depends on call history and, at ring capacity, it falsely admits every hallucinated export. The fix requires adding a read only `cgr_contains` query *to `congruence`* and having the ripple module use that, never mutating the audited ring. So a correctness fix in one forcefield module is really a capability addition to the shared congruence ring, and it improves the soundness of every ripple consumer that needs to ask "is this present" without side effect. Related: A-RL-1 and A-RC-1 both hoist a loop invariant `cg_decide` call across the extern boundary, which is an interaction cost (crossing the ABI per pair) rather than a correctness bug.

**RIPPLE-5 · `cad.iii` is the content address authority and the X23 lever audits every one of its callers.** The "swallowed `cad_oneshot` return" defect is a ripple waiting to be swept. When `cad_oneshot` receives a null or zero input it returns an error and *writes nothing* to its output buffer, so any caller that ignores the return then compares stale bytes and can be fooled into a false match. The audit found this in `commit_gate` (A-CG-1), `reach_oracle` (II-REACH_ORACLE-2, where it defeats a determinism firewall against forged pins), and `optinvoke` (A-OI-2, the domain separation variant). Lever `X23` is the instruction to audit *every* `cad_oneshot` and `*_oneshot` call site for a checked return. The interaction also runs the other way: `cad.iii` already appends a `0x00` domain separator, and lever `X4` asks `ripple` (A-RP-1) to *reuse* that existing separator rather than invent a new primitive, which is the same authority consumed correctly.

**RIPPLE-6 · `builder`'s push helpers thread through the whole output surface (X24).** `builder_push_byte` and `builder_push_bytes` return failure codes that many emitters discard, so a truncated artifact reads as complete. The affected callers form a wide fan: `http_server` and `http_client` (II-HTTPSERVER-3), `base32` (II-BASE32-1), `format` (II-FORMAT-1), `inet` (II-INET-1), and the `tp_*` codecs. Lever `X24` is to thread the builder's return to the public boundary everywhere. Like `cad`, `builder` is a shared service whose error contract is honored inconsistently, and the sweep is the ripple.

**RIPPLE-7 · `fe25519` is the field that `x25519` should stand on.** Already noted as COMBINE-9, but the interaction is worth isolating: today the two carry separate reduction logic, and `x25519`'s is both slower and, via E-X-3, not actually constant time. Routing `x25519` onto `fe25519` propagates both the speed and the side channel resistance from one module to the other. The dependency should point one way, the protocol module onto the field module.

**RIPPLE-8 · `cpufeat` gates every SIMD path, and `X17` is the cross machine determinism risk it creates.** `bigint` needs a new `cpufeat_has_avx512dq` check (D-BI-1, without which it crashes on AVX-512 F only CPUs like Knights Landing), and `sha256`, `sha512`, `chacha20`, and `mlkem` all gate accelerated paths on `cpufeat`. The systemic interaction is `X17`: these modules ship AVX-512 or AVX2 metal paths whose bit identity with the scalar fallback is asserted only in comments, never exercised, because the accelerated path never runs on a non accelerated continuous integration machine, so a lane constant typo would not redden the corpus. This is a determinism across machines hazard, the one place III's bit identical replay guarantee is taken on faith. The ripple opportunity is a single discipline applied through `cpufeat`'s dependents: a forced path KAT per accelerated module that asserts identical output across scalar, AVX2, and AVX-512 on a fixed vector.

**RIPPLE-9 · The `@specialize` mechanism propagates a sign extension bug to every signed element type (X20).** Findings J-RESULT-1 and J-ITER-4 show that for signed element types the generic specialization widens by sign extension, so a tagged payload using a shift fails to recover negative values, and J-ITER STRIDE-1 shows a related stride default. Because this lives in the specialization codegen rather than in any one container, it ripples to every `@specialize`d signed type across `result`, `iter`, and any future user. This is a codegen level interaction: fix the widening once at the specialization site and a whole family of latent container bugs closes.

## 3C. Edit here and that must rebuild (the seal and forge cascades)

**RIPPLE-10 · Editing any `COMPILER/BOOT/*.iii` drifts the bootstrap seal and requires `build_iiis2`.** The audit is emphatic and repeats it in several places: leaf STDLIB modules are seal neutral and gated by `build_stdlib` plus `run_corpus`, but the compiler's own source is bootstrap sealed, so an edit there drifts the iiis-2 seal and must pass the seal gate (byte equivalence on the stage one corpus, then a reseal to the golden BARE hash). The concrete trace is visible: the `cg_rm2` digit corruption fix changed the golden mhash from `4e1384...` to `53ce03...`, and the subsequent `cg_r3` slot fix moved it again to `9f64a2e6...`. The interaction principle is that the compiler files (`cg_rm2` for C9, `xii_ldil` for the STORE typecheck OOB, `sema` for G-SEMA-1 and 2) must be done *last*, in a dedicated seal gated pass, and even an output identical optimization like the `sema` hash index "still changes `sema.iii` source, so still gate via `build_iiis2`," because the seal covers source identity. Know this before touching anything under `COMPILER/BOOT`.

**RIPPLE-11 · The forge and manifest closure binds `katabasis/census`, `ring_lattice`, and `seal_resolver` to a three level content address cascade.** This is the deepest build time ripple, and it is why two findings were *correctly deferred*. `census.iii` is a forge sealed descent artifact whose whole file SHA feeds a three level closure: a per row seal, a SHA-256 descent sub closure root, and a Keccak manifest closure root. Editing it ripples to a forge reseal across all three levels, and the Keccak top root is "not cleanly recomputable in toolset," so a partial fix would leave the sovereign manifest half sealed, a breach of a hard invariant that the audit treats as a peer of the determinism gate, for a Low severity, in practice unreachable bug. Similarly `seal_resolver`'s coefficient table bytes are "the frozen seal definition" themselves, so correcting them is a spec level refreeze, a new ADR and reissue, not a code edit. The `ring_lattice` fix *was* landed, but only because its forge reseal was completed (the K6 root moved to `77a631...`). The interaction lesson: content address sealed artifacts cannot be edited as code; their edits are forge operations, and a fix that cannot complete the full closure recomputation should be deferred with evidence rather than applied half way.

## 3D. Change this and watch those (dispatch tables and lifecycle)

**RIPPLE-12 · `transform_patterns::tp_table_call` dispatches the X22 overflow to every `tp_*` codec.** The transform codec family shares one dispatch entry, `tp_table_call`, which forwards a caller supplied `src_len` to every codec. Since every codec guards its output capacity with an unchecked addition that wraps (lever `X22`: II-TP-MD-1, II-TP-ASTBIN-1, and the rest), the vulnerability is *dispatched* through one table to many bodies. The interaction means two things: the dispatch site is a natural place to add a single bound, and each codec still needs its own subtraction form guard, because the codecs are also reachable directly. Fixing the table alone is insufficient; fixing every codog body without the table is fragile. Both, with the table as the backstop.

**RIPPLE-13 · `reflection_constrained` and `reflection_governance` disagree on buffer sizes and rights.** Two interactions here. F-RFLC-2 shows `reflection_constrained` reads and writes past a 4,096 byte buffer that its caller `reflection_governance` sizes at only eighty bytes, a classic mismatch between a producer's assumption and a consumer's allocation. And F-RFLG-1 shows the W32 gate enforces an AMEND right while the dispatcher requires an ATTEST right, so a capability satisfying one fails the other, invisible in the selftest only because the root capability grants every bit. The combined lesson: where two modules cooperate across a buffer or a permission, the *contract* (size, right) must be a single shared constant, not two independent declarations that happen to agree until they do not.

**RIPPLE-14 · The "lazy init only from selftest" lifecycle couples public entries to a private populator (the X26 root cause).** This is a lifecycle interaction that the audit documents as a defect family but is worth naming as an interaction pattern. In `charter_terminal` (II-CHARTERTERMINAL-1, Critical), the public "one call, one verdict" entry folds a clause count that is zero until a *private* registration function runs, and that function is only reachable from the selftest, so a stranger calling the public entry gets an empty loop and a sealed false GREEN. The same shape recurs across the X26 set. The interaction is between a public entry and an initialization it assumes but does not perform. The fix family, making the entry self populating, is an enhancement (ENHANCE-17), but the structural observation is that III has a recurring coupling where the test path and the production path see different module states, and that gap is precisely where the vacuous gates live.

# 4. Capability and Utility Enhancement

The audit is dense with these, and they sort cleanly into four families: making things faster (constant factor and asymptotic), giving the system new primitives it lacks, *completing* the capabilities that are declared but only partially built, and hardening soundness so a gate can actually do its job. The completion family is the one the original audit's Volume II axis (implementation completeness) contributes that Volume I could not.

## 4A. Performance enhancements (the engine runs hotter)

**ENHANCE-1 · Incremental egraph rebuild.** F-CORE-1, lever `X8`. Today the egraph wipes its entire 262,144 slot hashcons on every rebuild pass and hashes every node into the table afresh, so a tiny graph still pays the full wipe per saturation step. The deferred rebuild technique from the "egg" line of work maintains a dirty worklist of touched classes and canonicalizes only the affected parents afresh, amortized to close to linear in the number of *touched* nodes. This is the dominant cost of saturation, so the win is large, and it preserves bit identical replay by draining the worklist in ascending class id order.

**ENHANCE-2 · The NTT tier for large multiplication.** COMBINE-1 and RIPPLE-2 as a capability: above the measured threshold, O(n log n) integer exact convolution replaces O(n^2) schoolbook, and it fixes the large operand correctness break as a side effect.

**ENHANCE-3 · Tropical Dijkstra extraction.** F-CORE-3 and COMBINE-5: replacing the full relaxation fixpoint with a priority ordered worklist that finalizes each class once converges in far fewer node touches, and it expresses the egraph measurement, the collapse of the superposition to the cheapest realization, as the explicit tropical shortest path the design narrative always intended.

**ENHANCE-4 · A real micro architectural cost field.** F-CORE-4 and SEPARATE-3: populating the six dimensional cost vector per ISA operation (latency, throughput, registers, instruction cache, data cache, energy) turns the headline "differential descent over the cost field" from a descent down a flat gradient into a genuine optimization that picks micro architecturally cheapest realizations, not merely fewest multiplies. The capability already has a data source in `microarch_model`.

**ENHANCE-5 · Knuth's division algorithm for bigint.** D-DIV-1: the current restoring bit serial division is O(bits times limbs) with a fresh allocation per bit; Knuth's Algorithm D is O(limbs times limbs), roughly sixty four times fewer inner operations, the single biggest constant factor win for RSA and ed25519. The module's own notes already anticipate it as "Phase E."

**ENHANCE-6 · The special form fold for x25519, a thousandfold.** E-X-1, RIPPLE-7: roughly a thousand times faster reduction by folding modulo `2^255 minus 19` instead of generic division, delivered by delegating to `fe25519`.

**ENHANCE-7 · Block oriented Keccak absorb, on the system's hottest path.** E2-KC-1. The streaming Keccak-256 absorb processes input one byte at a time, so a sixty four megabyte witness payload costs around sixty seven million iterations with roughly one hundred thirty six times the necessary branch and position work. The audit calls this "THE hot path" because every content address, identifier, witness fragment id, and synthesis key flows through it. Block oriented absorb, mirroring `keccak.iii`'s existing block routine, is bit identical and removes the per byte overhead. This pairs naturally with COMBINE-2: cheap hashing off the bucketing paths, and a fast correct Keccak on the seal paths that genuinely need it.

**ENHANCE-8 · Straus and Shamir double scalar verification, roughly twice as fast.** Lever `X16`, findings E-ED-1 and E-EC-1. Signature verification does two independent scalar multiplications then one addition; interleaving them into one doubling per bit with a four entry table select shares the doublings for about half the point operations. It is public verify side data, so the table select form raises no constant time concern, and it claims no complexity class speedup, only the honest constant factor.

**ENHANCE-9 · The constant factor sweep across the math and crypto cores.** A cluster of smaller but real wins: AES decrypt's `InvMixColumns` via xtime chains for roughly ten times fewer operations (E2-AES-2); `crc32` slice by 8 for four to eight times throughput (D-CRC-1); the `sha512` per block CPUID hoist, which today re runs sixteen full feature detects per block (E2-512-1); `fn256` and `fn384` needing fewer Newton iterations (D-FN-1, D-FN384-1); dedicated squaring and the `dbl-2008-hwcd` doubling formula in `fe25519` (E-FE-2, E-FE-3); Montgomery on the post quantum primes (E-MLK-2, E-MLD-2); and the `mlkem` and `mldsa` NTT run in place rather than with five hundred twelve copy moves (E-MLD-3).

**ENHANCE-10 · The structural efficiency wins in the support modules.** Stein's binary GCD to replace the subtractive Euclid that can hang on adversarial input (A-COSTLAT-1, a correctness *and* performance fix); the difference array sweep for register pressure, O(n) instead of O(n^2) (A-CC-1); the O(1) in flight counter that retires `ma_in_flight`'s per cycle scan (A-MA-1); CSR adjacency to turn the pleroma gauge fix from O(V times E) into O(V plus E) (A-PL-1); the memo lattice probe that stops at the resolved slot instead of always iterating sixty five thousand times (A-ML-1); the `pq` hole sift (A-PQ-1); the math library O(1) admission (D-ML-1); memoization for the uncertainty DAG, O(2^k) to O(V plus E) (F-UNC-1); Buchberger's second criterion to prune S pairs in the Groebner engine (F-GB-2); the optimized bracket abstraction in `combinator` to stop exponential output growth (F-CB-1); and the compiler's own `sema` name resolution hash index (G-SEMA-1) and Aho and Corasick opcode scan (G-SEMA-2).

## 4B. New primitives the system lacks

**ENHANCE-11 · Substream splitting for `xoshiro`.** D-XO-2: adding `jump` and `long_jump` (computed from a polynomial of the state matrix over GF(2)) gives exact, non overlapping substream splitting at the `2^128` scale, a capability the deterministic substrate would use wherever it needs independent yet reproducible streams.

**ENHANCE-12 · Wide bit blasting beyond six variables.** The `bv_ring` honest ceiling note: extending the sixty four wide single register all valuations decider to a column stack of u64 words for more than six variables stays exact and broadens the SAT capability without leaving the deterministic gate.

**ENHANCE-13 · Wiring the proof carrying code primitive behind the optimizer.** The audit observes that `zk_snark`'s Groth16 *is* the proof carrying code primitive the gate calls for, and explicitly recommends wiring it behind the optimizer proposal pipeline. This is a latent capability already built and not yet connected, the highest kind of return.

## 4C. Completing what is declared (the implementation completeness axis)

**ENHANCE-14 · The crypto inlines, generated or removed.** The top completion finding, `X25`. The `xii_curated_*` inlines are scaffolds; the completion is to generate them from the proven `numera` crypto so the inline equals the verified algorithm, with a per horizon KAT asserting equality on a fixed vector, or to remove the override entirely. Until then, the audit's standing instruction holds: these must not be wired into any executed crypto.
> **[CORRECTED 2026-05-29 — ALREADY_FIXED]** The override-precedence defect this depends on is **closed** (`xii_emit_gen.iii:258`); no curated crypto stub can be emitted today. This is now pure *completion* (generate inlines from proven `numera` crypto + per-horizon equality KAT), not a guard against a live hazard.

**ENHANCE-15 · The transform slots, implemented or relabeled.** The `tp_*` family over claims. Several slots name a transpiler but deliver a source embed or a byte directive emit: `tp_iii_to_c99` and `tp_iii_to_latex` embed source rather than lowering it, `tp_x86_disasm` emits byte directives rather than decoding instructions, `tp_asm_to_pe` emits a DOS stub and signature but not a loadable PE32+ (the optional header and section are all zero), and `transform` itself is dispatched to convert zero bytes while `transform_buffer` truncates to eight bytes (II-TRANSFORM-1/2, II-TP-ASM2PE-1, II-TP-ASTBIN faithfulness notes). The completion is a deliberate design decision per slot: build the real transform, a genuine x86 decoder, a valid PE32+ optional header and populated section, a real III to C99 lowering, or rename the slot to its true capability so the spec stops over claiming. Either is honest; silence is not.

**ENHANCE-16 · The partially built capabilities in the dynamic and resolution layers.** `dynamic_impact`'s declared `aggregate_ux` is unimplemented and its `aggregate_perf` is a stub of its contract, with a sign bug on negative inputs (II-DYNIMP-1/2/3); `resolution_init` always returns success even on a failed registration step (II-RESINIT-1); `base32`'s decoder accepts illegal pad counts and so decodes malformed input "successfully" (II-BASE32-2). Each is a declared capability that the body does not fully deliver, and each completion is concrete and local.

**ENHANCE-17 · The vacuous gates, made load bearing (X26, the system wide completion).** The most common Volume II defect class is a gate that returns valid over an empty or unverified state and therefore cannot say no. The completion, per gate, is to make the return depend on the real computation and ship a falsifier KAT that drives a bad input to a rejection. The set is large and named: `charter_terminal` (empty registry to GREEN, made self populating), `proof_ripple_resolution` (always returns one, never recomputes the roots), `irreducibility_proof` (always OK, always counts to three hundred sixty one), `quality_q7` (a bare boolean with no backing lint), `babel_intent` (validates by finding a three byte substring), `resolver_memo` and `resolver_replay` (guards never exercised), `xii_curate` (the same ceremony id finalizes twelve times), `mandate` (reports satisfied for a dead chain id), and `pattern_set_federation` (a fetch that omits its own ancestry check). This family is enhancement and removal at once: each gate gains the *capability to reject*, which is the only capability a gate has that matters.

## 4D. Soundness hardening

**ENHANCE-18 · Domain separated and arity framed Merkle hashing.** Lever `X4`, finding A-RP-1. The `ripple` Merkle DAG has no domain separation between leaf values and interior nodes and no length framing, so a leaf value equal to a child address yields the same address as a one input computed cell, a second preimage and leaf as internal node confusion that contradicts the "same inputs, and only that, give the same root" claim. Tagging leaves against nodes and prefixing an explicit arity closes a whole class of aliasing, and `cad`'s existing separator means no new primitive is needed (RIPPLE-5).

**ENHANCE-19 · Constant time claims matched to reality.** Lever `X12`. The `@constant_time` and `@side_channel_resistant` annotations are aspirational where data dependent branches remain: `x25519`'s swap branches on the scalar bit (E-X-3), `fp256`'s borrow branches on secret operands (D-FP-1), and `aes` indexes its S box by a secret and branches in its field multiply (E2-AES-1). The hardening is branch free masked operations over a data independent field, or, where that is larger work, dropping the annotation until it holds, because an unfounded constant time assertion is worse than an honest absence.

**ENHANCE-20 · Standards conformance and canonical encoding.** Lever `X15`. `slhdsa` carries a non FIPS-205 hash instantiation under a FIPS-205 label, so it will not interoperate (E-SLH-2); `ecdsa_p256` never checks that its signature components lie in range, accepting malleable signatures (E-EC-2); `fe25519` is missing the RFC 8032 canonical decode checks (E-FE-4). The hardening is to conform or to correct the claim, the same honesty principle as the transform relabeling.

**ENHANCE-21 · A permanent independent conversion oracle for the kernel.** II-CCL discussion, and SEPARATE-2: keep `combinator.iii`'s `cb_conv` as a *permanent* differential check against the CCL conversion path, so a soundness bug in the reducer must also fool the combinator path to escape. This is a capability the system already has and should commit to keeping rather than retiring, a soundness hedge of the highest standard.

**ENHANCE-22 · Degenerate cases that fail rather than emit invalid output.** Lever `X14`. RSA keygen ships a dead key (d equals zero) as success when the totient is divisible by the public exponent (E-RSA-1); ECDSA does not retry on a degenerate zero component (E-EC-3); the post quantum dispatcher underflows on a zero suite low nibble (E-PQD-2). The hardening is uniform: reject or retry, never proceed with an invalid artifact.

**ENHANCE-23 · The soundness micro fixes worth naming.** Making `merkle`'s currently dead `cur_idx` load bearing so it binds the leaf index into verification, which *strengthens* soundness (MERKLE-2); modeling `bv_ring`'s shift by width to match the x86 mask so a rewrite is not certified against semantics the target does not honor (C-BV-1); domain separating the polynomial commitment leaves by index so the commitment is position binding rather than a multiset proof (C-PC-3); and making `nous_search` distinguish an oracle capacity error from a genuine "no answer exists," so a resource error is never reported as a trusted refutation (C-NS-1).

# 5. "This is junk. Get this out of here."

Removal divides into three honest categories: *dead matter* that is simply unused and should be deleted, *dangerous matter* that is worse than dead because it actively misleads or fails open and must be removed or replaced, and a short *do not touch* list of things that look removable but are load bearing by virtue of being sealed. The third category is as important as the first two, because the fastest way to break a content address closure is to delete a byte that a seal is defined over.

## 5A. Dead matter (delete)

**CUT-1 · `TL_VAL_FILLED`, four megabytes of dead BSS.** C-TL-2. In `temporal_logic`, this array is allocated, cleared on an O(subformulas times length) loop, and written per cell, yet never consumed. Deleting it halves the module's BSS from eight megabytes to four and removes the clear loop. The single largest dead allocation in the audit.

**CUT-2 · Dead constants in the cost lattice synthesizer.** A-CLS-4. `CLS_DIM_RESERVED` and `CLS_DESC_BYTES` are dead; the packer hardcodes the value the constant should provide. Either wire the constant in as the single source of truth or delete both.

**CUT-3 · `ma_in_flight`, retired by an O(1) counter.** A-MA-1. The per cycle scan of issued and retired arrays in `microarch_model` is replaceable by a counter subtraction, and the function should then be deleted outright.

**CUT-4 · The unreachable return in `ripple_unify`.** A-RU-1. After a certified union returns success, the subsequent find always agrees, so the final `return 0` is unreachable; it should be a direct `return 1`, and the dead branch removed so it stops reading as a live guard.

**CUT-5 · `XJN_NJ`, dead BSS in the joinability module.** B-JN-2. Unused; delete.

**CUT-6 · The dead fixed point scale constants.** D-FX-4. `FX16_SCALE`, `FX24_SCALE`, and `FX48_SCALE` in `fixed_extra` are dead. Remove or use.

**CUT-7 · The redundant Massey comparison in `galois`.** D-GAL-2. Dead and redundant; delete.

**CUT-8 · The dead u32 masks (the X9 sweep).** A whole anti bloat family: `& 0xFFFFFFFF` masks on values that already wrap as u32, in `scalar` (D-SC-2), `checked` (D-CHK-4), `murmur3` (D-MUR-2), `fold`, `either` (alongside its false "u31" comment), and `sha256_dispatch` (which also carries a dead `_selected`). These are individually trivial and collectively a clean sweep that removes noise from the reader's path. The audit gathers them under lever `X9` for exactly this reason.

**CUT-9 · Dead compiler bookkeeping in `cg_r3`.** The DEAD-D7-1 duplicate label gate and the DEAD-STACK-1 `R3_G_MAX_STACK_DEPTH`. Delete or wire. Note that this lives in a bootstrap sealed file, so removal rides the seal gate (RIPPLE-10), which is why it is low priority despite being genuine dead matter.

**CUT-10 · The dead extern and stale labels.** `fold`'s dead `iter_u8_remaining` extern; `nous_value`'s stale "49 by 49 selection sort" label where the real work is linear; the retired rule references that no longer denote anything live: `nous_socket` carrying R001 through R004 in its order tables (wasting compares per node) and `xii_joinability` testing against the retired R001 through R003 range (B-JN-1). These are dead *references*, citations to things that have been removed, and they should be re expressed in the live rule ids or deleted.

**CUT-11 · The certificate that certifies nothing.** `nous_completion`'s "convergence certificate" is the constant hash of a single zero byte, so it binds nothing. This straddles dead and dangerous: as written it is dead (it carries no information), but because it is *named* a certificate it is mildly dangerous (it implies a guarantee it does not provide). The fix is to make it bind the actual pair count and budget, or to remove the pretense.

# 6. Synthesis: the architecture the findings imply

Five higher order observations follow from the whole, and they are the part of this pass that goes beyond reorganizing the audit. Each is a claim about III's architecture that no single finding states but that the body of findings, read together, makes hard to avoid.

**The missing primitive layer is the system's largest structural debt, and it is invisible at the file level.** Six of the cross cutting levers (`X1`, `X2`, `X3`, `X5`, `X11`, `X21`) are each a different face of one fact: III hand rolls the same small set of deterministic superposition primitives independently across many files. A content address index, a tie break authority, a min plus priority queue, a modular reduction, an NTT convolution, a fast bucketing hash. The audit proposes each as a separate lever because it found them in separate files. The synthesis is that they form a coherent *layer*, and that the highest leverage architectural move in the system is not any one of them but the decision to treat them as one layer that every consumer depends on. The reason this debt is invisible at the file level is precisely the NIH discipline's virtue turned vice: because files are small and self contained, no single file looks bloated, and the duplication only becomes visible when you read across the whole tree, which is what the audit's lever series quietly did.

**The boundary is the place where III is least like itself.** III's interior is exact, deterministic, proof carrying, and bit identical. Its boundary, the `@export` surface where caller supplied lengths and ids and untrusted bytes arrive, is none of those things: it masks ids into aliases (`X19`), adds lengths that wrap (`X18`, `X22`), overloads sentinels (`X6`), and swallows errors (`X23`, `X24`). The seven levers that name these are one finding: the interior's guarantees are not enforced *at the door*. This is the natural failure mode of a system whose discipline is internal, because the discipline was never expressed as a boundary contract. The remedy (COMBINE-7) is a thin boundary layer, and the deeper point is that a deterministic system needs its determinism defended at the perimeter as rigorously as in the core, because an out of bounds write driven by a network byte (II-HTTPSERVER-1) defeats every interior guarantee at once.

**The trusted base is larger than the narrative, and that gap is the only place soundness can hide.** The system says `typecheck` is the arbiter of meaning. The findings say the arbiter is `ccl` plus a translation layer, a much larger surface, and that surface carried three soundness defects until they were closed. The architectural lesson is not that the defects existed; it is that the *boundary of the trusted base was not drawn where the narrative claimed*, and unexamined trusted code is exactly where an unsoundness can live undetected (II-CCL-2's false convertibility on arena exhaustion is the proof). Drawing that boundary explicitly (SEPARATE-2), keeping a second independent oracle to guard it (ENHANCE-21), and making confluence a theorem rather than a sample (II-CCL-3) are the three moves that align the architecture with its own claim. A system that proves things must be most rigorous about what it does *not* prove, and the trusted base is precisely that frontier.

**There are two implementations of several things, and the shadow always wins.** Crypto exists twice and the stub set takes precedence (`X25`). `checked` duplicates `option`'s table. `x25519` duplicates `fe25519`'s field. The NTT exists three times. This is the single source of truth principle violated, and the curated crypto case shows why it is dangerous rather than merely untidy: when two implementations exist and a precedence mechanism chooses between them, the wrong one can silently win, and a tautological verify ends up on the live path. The synthesis is that III should hold one implementation of each capability and *generate* any specialized form (an inline, a SIMD path) from that one source, so the specialized form is provably the same algorithm. The X25 plan, generate the inlines from the proven routines, is the template for this principle everywhere it applies.
> **[CORRECTED 2026-05-29]** Reword the present tense: in the crypto case the shadow *did* win **until X25 closed it** (`xii_emit_gen.iii:258`). Three of the four instances (`checked`/`option`, `x25519`/`fe25519`, the now-**four** NTTs) still persist, so the *principle* holds; the crypto *instance* is past tense and is the proven template.

**The vacuous gate epidemic is a lifecycle bug wearing a correctness costume.** The most common Volume II defect, a gate that returns GREEN over an empty registry, is repeatedly caused by the same lifecycle coupling: the registry is populated by a private function reachable only from the selftest, so the production path and the test path see different module states (RIPPLE-14). The gates are not individually wrong so much as *unwitnessed*, and the audit's prescription, a falsifier KAT per gate proving it can reject (`X26`, the system wide form of Volume I's `X10`), is really a demand that every gate's *negative* behavior be exercised. The deepest version of this is the discipline the audit applies to itself and names "prove the negative": a guarantee you have only ever seen succeed is not a guarantee, it is an untested hope, and the difference between the two is exactly a test that drives the bad input to the rejection. That principle, more than any single fix, is the one to carry into the uncovered work and into anything built on III hereafter.
